Aloo Chaat

Medley of Indian spices on the most humble vegetable of all time, potato. Aloo chaat is a nifty snack that can be prepared in less than 10 minutes. Make this tangy Indian snack at home for a good time.

Ingredients

  • 250 gms Potato

For chaat

  • 1/4 tsp black pepper
  • 1/2 tsp red chilli
  • 1/2 tsp roasted cumin powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon dry mango powder (amchur powder)
  • Black salt/white salt as per taste
  • 1 tbsp finely chopped mint leaves
  • 2 tbsp finely chopped coriander leaves
  • 1 green chilli (finely chopped)

steps

  • Peel and dice the potato in small finger-sized pieces.
  • Fry them under medium-low heat until golden brown.
  • Toss them in a bowl and add all the ingredients listed above under "For Chaat".
  • Give the mix a nice stir and serve hot.

Dahi Puri

Pani puri with the "pani" that splatters is another great idea for a quick snack. Super easy to make and once prepped, ingredients can be used over and over again until your heart is fulfilled.

Ingredients

  • 8 Puris (Gol Gappa)
  • 1/2 cup boiled & chopped potatoes
  • 1/4 cup chopped onion
  • 2 & 1/2 tbsp tamarind chutney
  • 1 & 1/2 tsp green chutney
  • 1/2 tsp garlic chutney (optional)
  • 1/2 cup beaten curd
  • 1/4 cup sev
  • Chopped coriander to garnish

steps

  • Drizzle 1 tsp tamarin chutney, 1/2 tsp green chutney and 1/4 tsp garlic chutney over it.
  • Break open the puris and add 1 tbsp chopped and boiled potatoes and 1/2 tbsp chopped onions to each of them.
  • Pour 1 tbsp curd and sprinkle some sev and coriander leaves on each puri. Voila, your dahi puris are ready to eat.

Paneer Pakoras

Any paneer dish is always a great grub. This quick Indian snack recipe doesn't take more than 10 minutes but the effects last for a lifetime. It's also a good conversation starter for when guests are over.

Ingredients

  • 150 grams of paneer cut into squares
  • 1 cup besan
  • 1 pinch turmeric powder
  • 1 to 2 pinches of red chilli powder or cayenne pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon carom seeds (ajwain)
  • 1 to 2 pinches of garam masala powder
  • 1 pinch asafetida (hing)
  • salt as required
  • 2/3 to 3/4 cup water

steps

  • Take a bowl and add 1 cup besan.
  • Now add turmeric powder, red chilli powder, carom seeds, garam masala, hing and salt as per the instructions.
  • Give it a proper whisk and start pouring water in parts.
  • Now follow the mix and cut technique to make a slightly thick batter.

Bhel Puri

I've seen my dad fix a delish plate of bhel puri every time he is craving spicy street food. You can add almost anything and it still tastes like a warm hug.

Ingredients

  • 2 cup murmura / puffed rice
  • 1/2 onion (finely chopped)
  • 1/2 potato / aloo (boiled & cubed)
  • 3 papdi (crushed)
  • 3 tbsp mixture
  • 2 tbsp fried peanuts
  • 2 tbsp tomato (finely chopped)
  • 1/2 tsp chaat masala
  • 1/4 tsp red chilli powder
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 3 tbsp tamarind chutney
  • 2 tbsp green chutney
  • 1 tsp lemon juice
  • 2 tbsp sev
  • 1 tsp coriander

steps

  • In a large mixing bowl take 2 cups murmura.
  • Add 1/2 onion, 1/2 potato, 3 crushed papdi, 3 tbsp mixture and 2 tbsp fried peanuts.
  • Also add 2 tbsp tomato, 1/2 tsp chaat masala, 1/4 tsp chilli powder and 1/4 tsp salt.
  • Mix well to make sure the spices are combined well.
  • Further add 3 tbsp tamarind chutney, 2 tbsp green chutney and 1 tsp lemon juice.
  • Add 2 tbsp sev and give a mix.
  • Finally, enjoy bhel puri topped with a few crushed papdi and garnished with onion, coriander.

Poha

Originally a breakfast dish, Poha is also a delectable Indian snack that can be cooked in 10 minutes. But the best part about this recipe is - as soon as you get the hang of it, you can prep and eat it under those 10 mins.

Ingredients

  • 1½ cup poha/flattened rice
  • 1 onion medium
  • 12-14 curry leaves
  • 2 green chilli
  • ¼ cup raw peanuts
  • 1 teaspoon mustard/rai seeds
  • ¼ teaspoon turmeric/haldi powder
  • 2 tablespoons refined oil
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • Fresh coriander for garnishing
  • salt to taste

steps

  • In a sieve add poha and rinse it thoroughly with water. Add some sugar and gently give it a mix.
  • Let the poha rest for some time, till we prepare the rest of the ingredients.
  • In a pan over medium flame, add raw peanuts and dry roast them for 3-4 minutes or until it turns aromatic and gets a light golden colour.
  • Transfer the peanuts to a bowl and in the same pan add oil and once it turns hot add the mustard seeds and once they pop add chopped onions and saute them for a minute.
  • Now add some curry leave and saute for another 2-3 minutes or until the onions get a light golden colour.
  • Add turmeric and cook for a minute. Finally, add the roasted peanuts and bloomed poha and gently give it a mix. Don’t over mix else the poha might break.
  • Cover it with a lid and cook for 2 minutes on low-medium flame.
  • Once done, top it with some fresh coriander and lemon juice and serve hot.
